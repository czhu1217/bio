About me

I’m currently a second year PhD student at Johns Hopkins School of Medicine with a focus on supplementary and alternative medicine. I come from a Chinese back ground and my grand father was a traditional Chinese physicist, so I’ve had much exposure to non-conventional medicines since young. Quite a few people around me prefer alternative approaches such as naturopathy instead of conventional western medicine, and preferring “natural” medication has become a trend among many people. However, since the second year of undergrad, I’ve noticed a lack of research that proves the effectiveness of such approaches. That’s why I chose to focus on it for my PhD study. This blog is my side project in an effort to introduce the basics of naturopathy and my opinions on it as a medical student.
