Conclusion on Naturopathy

2021-03-24

After a brief explanation of various parts of natural remedies, there’s no doubt that they improve our health in certain cases, however, I don’t completely agree that they are effective and a safer alternative to pharmaceuticals, nor should it be used instead of pharmaceuticals or conventional western medical treatments.

First of all, the effectiveness of almost all the naturopathy treatment methods remains unverified for many diseases they are used for. For instance, there’s no evidence that extremely diluted products used in homeopathy will have any effect. In fact, it contradicts fundamental scientific principles. It is very likely that in such cases the “improvement” in health people experience are placebo effects. Some approaches are effective toward certain diseases but are generalized to other ones by common sense, which makes their effectiveness questionable as well. For example, osteopathy is proven effective for acute lower back pain, but no proof has been shown that it’s effective for neck pain or sport injuries.

Secondly, they are not necessarily safer either. We talked about herb-drug interaction before, and how it’s more dangerous than drug-drug interactions. For instance, St John’s wort is banned in France and only available in other countries with a prescription because of its serious interactions with some medications. Although the name “naturopathy” may give the approach a harmless and “natural” impression, they are not necessarily safer and require extra precaution when consuming.

There are much research left to be done to evaluate the effectiveness of these approaches, and before that is done, conventional western medical treatments is still the preferred approach to diseases because they have sufficient scientific backup.
