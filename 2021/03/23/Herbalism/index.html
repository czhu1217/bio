Herbalism

2021-03-23

In the last journal I discussed three treatment methods of naturopathy, today we will talk about herbalism, which is used to treat disease in naturopathy.

There are many herbs being used, I will use 3 examples: oregano oil, St John’s wort and stringing nettle.

Oregano oil

Oregano oil is usually used for respiratory tract disorders such as coughs, croup, asthma, and bronchitis. It’s also used for gastrointestinal disorders such as heartburn and bloating.

St. John’s Wort

St. John’s Wort is used to treat mild to moderate depression, mood disorder, and symptoms of menopause.

Stinging Nettle

Stinging nettle is commonly used to treat diabetes and osteoarthritis. In some cases it’s also used for urinary tract infections, kidney stone, and muscle pain.

Herb-Drug Interaction

In the herbs mentioned above, St. John’s wort has been known for having severe herb-drug interactions. herb-drug interactions are the concurrent use of herbs that may mimic, magnify, or oppose the effect of drugs. In some cases they are more dangerous than drug-drug interactions because there’s little research and clinical proof that explores herb-drug interactions. Herb products are also usually composed of more than one active ingredient, which increases their probability of interactions.
